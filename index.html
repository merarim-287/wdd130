Baja California, Mexico

Baja California is a state in the north of Mexico, with an estimated population of 3,769,020 as of 2020. It is the 12th largest state by area in Mexico. It's climate varies from Mediterranean to arid. The Mediterranean climate is found in the norhtwestern corner of the state, where the summers are dry and mild, and the winters are cool and rainy. Commmon trees in the high elevations are the Jeffrey pine, sugar pine and pinon pine. Baja California offers one of the best educational programs in the country, with high rankings in schooling and achievemenet. The state government provides educaton and qualification courses to increase the workforce standards, such as schoool-enterprice linkage programs which help the development of labor force according to the needs of the industry. Baja California N. is often referred to as Baja California Norte to distinguish it from both the Baja California peninsula as a whole, of which it forms the northern half, and Baja California Sur, the adjacent state that covers the southern half of the peninsula.
